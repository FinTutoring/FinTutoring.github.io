Key Formulas by CFA® Domain

Quantitative Methods (6-9%)

Time Value of Money

FV = PV(1 + r)ⁿ

PV = FV / (1 + r)ⁿ

🧠 Mnemonic: "Future Vault = Present Vault × Growth Magic"

Average Returns

Arithmetic Mean = Σr / n

Geometric Mean = [(1+r₁)×...×(1+rₙ)]^(1/n) - 1

📊 Relation: Arithmetic > Geometric > Harmonic

Fixed Income (11-14%)

Bond Pricing

P = Σ[CFₜ / (1 + r)ᵗ]

YTM approximation ≈ (C + (FV-P)/n) / ((FV+P)/2)

💡 Tip: Price ↑ when Rates ↓

Duration & Sensitivity

Modified Duration = Macaulay Duration / (1 + YTM)

%ΔP ≈ -Duration × ΔYTM

🔗 Link: Duration = "price elasticity to rates"

Equity Investments (11-14%)

Dividend Discount Model

P = D₁ / (r - g)

P/E = D₁/E₁ / (r - g)

💰 Condition: r > g (rate > growth)

Valuation Ratios

ROE = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity

P/B = P/E × ROE

🎯 Mnemonic: "Price-Book = Price-Earnings × Return-Equity"

Portfolio Management (8-12%)

Portfolio Return & Risk

E(Rₚ) = Σwᵢ × E(Rᵢ)

σₚ² = Σwᵢ²σᵢ² + 2ΣΣwᵢwⱼCov(i,j)

⚖️ Weights × Individual Returns

CAPM & Beta

E(Rᵢ) = Rₓ + βᵢ[E(Rₘ) - Rₓ]

β = Cov(Rᵢ,Rₘ) / Var(Rₘ)

📈 Beta > 1 : riskier than market

Practical Example: TVM Question

Typical CFA® Question:

"An investor invests €10,000 at 6% annual compound interest. What will be the value in 5 years?"

A) €13,382 • B) €13,000 • C) €12,625

Step 1: Identification (15s)

Keywords: "compound interest", "5 years"
→ TVM Formula: FV = PV(1+r)ⁿ

Step 2: Calculation (45s)

PV = 10,000, r = 6%, n = 5
FV = 10,000 × (1.06)⁵
FV = 10,000 × 1.3382 = €13,382

Step 3: Validation (30s)

Verification: 13,382 > 13,000 ✓
Consistency with 6% growth ✓
→ Answer A

Exam Strategies & Avoiding Pitfalls

Common Pitfalls & Solutions

❌ Frequent Errors

Sign Confusion:

Negative PV = cash outflow, Positive FV = cash inflow

Time Units:

Always align I/Y and N on the same basis (annual/monthly)

Calculator Mode:

Check P/Y = 1 for simple annual calculations

✅ Validation Strategies

Consistency Test:

FV > PV if positive rate, logical relationship
