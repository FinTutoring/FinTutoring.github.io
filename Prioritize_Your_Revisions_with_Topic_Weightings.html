Prioritize your revisions with topic weightings

A methodological guide to optimize your study time and maximize your chances of exam success

Introduction: The art of smart studying

Faced with a dense exam syllabus and tight deadlines, knowing how to prioritize your revisions becomes a decisive advantage. The weighting method allows you to allocate study time strategically according to each subject’s relative importance in the final evaluation.

This data-driven approach transforms exam preparation into a scientific process where every hour invested yields a measurable return on investment in potential points.

Understanding weightings

What is a weighting?

The weighting represents the relative importance of a subject in the final grade. A topic weighted at 30% contributes three times more to the result than one weighted at 10%.

Where to find this information?

  • • Official exam syllabus
  • • Candidate guide
  • • Past papers from previous years
  • • Specialized forums and experience feedback

The prioritization matrix

4-step methodology:

1. Potential Impact

Subject weighting × Probability of appearing in the exam

2. Difficulty Level

Subjective complexity of the topic (scale 1–5)

3. Time Required

Estimated hours needed to master the topic

4. Priority Score

Impact / (Difficulty × Time) = Efficiency ratio

Priority classifications:

Priority 1: High weighting + Relative ease (Quick wins)
Priority 2: Medium weighting + Moderate difficulty
Priority 3: Low weighting or very high difficulty

Strategic revision planning

Phase 1: Foundation

  • • Priority 1 topics (60% of time)
  • • Consolidating basics
  • • Typical exercises and past papers
  • • Summary sheets

Phase 2: Extension

  • • Priority 2 topics (30% of time)
  • • Selective deepening
  • • Connections between topics
  • • Advanced case studies

Phase 3: Excellence

  • • Priority 3 topics (10% of time)
  • • Potential bonus points
  • • Topics of personal interest
  • • Detailed refinement

Optimization techniques

Time management

  • Adapted Pomodoro technique: 45min study + 15min break
  • Thematic blocks: Group related topics
  • Spaced repetition: Review according to the forgetting curve
  • Peak performance: Tackle hard topics during optimal hours

Cognitive methods

  • Active recall: Test your memory without support
  • Elaborative interrogation: Ask yourself “why”
  • Interleaving: Alternate between different problem types
  • Dual coding: Combine visual and textual
